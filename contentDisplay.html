Distributed Consensus: The Raft Protocol

calendar_today Oct 12, 2023 person D. Ongaro, J. Ousterhout verified Peer Reviewed

Distributed consensus is a fundamental problem in modern cloud infrastructure. For a cluster of servers to act as a single, coherent unit, they must agree on the state of data even in the presence of network partitions or hardware failures. While Paxos was the dominant protocol for decades, its complexity led to a search for more understandable alternatives.

Leader Election Sub-Protocol

Raft decomposes consensus into independent sub-problems. The leader election process ensures that at most one leader exists for any given term. If a follower receives no communication over a period of time called the election timeout, it transitions to a candidate state and requests votes from peers.

  • Heartbeat mechanism prevents unnecessary re-elections during stable periods.
  • Randomized election timeouts ensure minimal split-vote scenarios.
  • Candidates must prove their logs are at least as up-to-date as the majority to win.

Log Replication Dynamics

Once a leader is elected, it begins servicing client requests. Each request contains a command to be executed by the replicated state machines. The leader appends the command to its log as a new entry, then issues AppendEntries RPCs in parallel to each of the other servers to replicate the entry.

Safety & Verification

Raft ensures safety by guaranteeing that if a leader has committed a log entry for a particular index, that entry will be present in the logs of all future leaders. This is verified through a formal proof of the State Machine Safety property.

P(L_t \cap L_{t+1}) \implies Log(L_t) \subseteq Log(L_{t+1})
The Leader Completeness Property ensures committed entries persist through terms.
S_i = \{e \in Log | term(e) \le term(Leader) \}

The transition between server states (Follower, Candidate, Leader) is governed by strict timeout rules and monotonic term numbering, creating a robust, mathematically verifiable framework for distributed reliability.
